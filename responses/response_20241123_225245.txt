Question: if i have 3 sisters and so does my neighbor and he also has 2 brothers and so do i how many kids are there combined in both houses?

Answer:

- You have 3 sisters.
- You have 2 brothers.
- Your neighbor also has 3 sisters and 2 brothers.

Now, let's count the number of children in each household:

**Your Household:**
- 1 (you)
- 3 sisters
- 2 brothers

So, your household has a total of **6** children (including yourself).

**Neighbor's Household:**
- 1 (your neighbor)
- 3 sisters
- 2 brothers

So, your neighbor’s household also has a total of **6** children (including the neighbor themselves).

**Combined Total:**

To find the combined total number of kids in both houses:
- Your household: 6
- Neighbor's household: 6

Thus, there are a **total of 12** kids combined in both households.
