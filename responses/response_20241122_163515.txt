Question: if sally has 4 brothers and 7 sisters but her sister also has 7 sisters and each of her brothers has 3 other brothers and her neighbors have the same amount of kids how many kids in total are in both houses?

Answer:
Let's break it down:

**Sally's family:**

* Sally has 4 brothers.
* Sally has 7 sisters.

So, Sally is one of the 11 children (4 brothers + 7 sisters).

Since each of her brothers has 3 other brothers, that means there are at least 9 siblings in total for Sally's family (not counting herself). This would mean:

* The remaining 2 siblings are Sally's sisters.
* However, we know Sally's sister also has 7 sisters. So, the only explanation is that Sally and her sister live in two separate households.

**Her sister's family:**

* Her sister also has 7 sisters. This means she has at least 1 brother, since every child must have a sibling.
* We know each of her brothers has 3 other brothers.

Let's count the number of siblings:

Sally's family (4 brothers + 6 sisters): 10
Her sister's family (7 sisters + 1 brother = 8)

The total number of kids in Sally's house is: **10**
The total number of kids in her sister's house is also: **8**

Since both houses have the same amount of kids, we can conclude that:

**Total number of kids in both houses:** **18**

This answer assumes that the neighbors refer to siblings who live in separate households.
